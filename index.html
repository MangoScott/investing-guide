Core Concept

The Power of
Compound Interest

Einstein called it "the eighth wonder of the world." Those who understand it, earn it. Those who don't, pay it.

The Compound Interest Formula

A = P(1 + r/n)nt
A = Final Amount
P = Principal
r = Interest Rate
n = Compounds/Year
t = Time (years)

Interest on Interest

Compound interest doesn't just pay you on your initial investment—it pays you on all the interest you've already earned. This exponential growth is the secret to building substantial wealth over time.

Mental Math

The Rule of 72

A simple shortcut to estimate how long it takes your money to double

72 ÷ Interest Rate = Years to Double

Example: 72 ÷ 6% = 12 years

Avoid These

Three Fatal
Investment Mistakes

Common pitfalls that destroy wealth

Ignoring Expenses

A 1% fee difference can cost hundreds of thousands over a lifetime.

  • Always choose no-load funds
  • Keep expense ratios under 0.25%
  • Avoid 12b-1 fees and sales charges

Chasing Performance

Last year's winners are often next year's losers.

  • Stick with broad diversification
  • Rebalance annually
  • Focus on asset allocation

Emotional Investing

Panic selling and greedy buying destroys wealth.

  • Create a plan and stick to it
  • Use dollar-cost averaging
  • Think long-term (20+ years)

Research Guide

Using Morningstar
to Evaluate Funds

Step-by-step instructions for morningstar.com

Step 1

Enter Ticker Symbol

Type the fund's ticker symbol in the search box at the top of morningstar.com

Step 2

Quote Tab - Category

Find the fund's category (e.g., "Large Blend", "Technology", "Small Value")

Step 3

People Tab - Manager Tenure

Check how long the current manager has run the fund. Longer tenure = more reliable track record

Step 4

Price Tab - Loads & Fees

Maximum Sales Fees: Should show "—" (no load)

Expense Ratio: Keep under 1.00%

Step 5

Performance Tab - Returns

Scroll to "Trailing Returns" section. Record 1-month, 1-year, 3-year, 5-year, and 10-year returns

Step 6

Performance Tab - Star Rating

Look for stars next to ticker symbol. 4-5 stars = good, 1-2 stars = poor

Step 7

Sustainability Tab - ESG Score

Find "Historical Sustainability Score Percent Rank". Lower % = better (less ESG risk)

Lipper Scores

Five-Factor Rating

Each fund gets 5 separate Lipper scores (1-5 scale):

  • Total Return - Overall performance
  • Consistent Return - Reliability
  • Preservation - Capital protection
  • Expense - Fee efficiency
  • Tax Efficiency - After-tax returns
Risk Metrics

Standard Deviation

Found in the "Risk" tab

Measures consistency of returns:

  • Low (1-10): Stable, predictable
  • Medium (10-20): Moderate swings
  • High (20+): Volatile, risky

Key Considerations

For long-term IRA investors, focus on:

  • Total Return (Lipper #1)
  • Consistent Return (Lipper #2)
  • Expense (Lipper #4)

Tax efficiency matters less in tax-advantaged accounts like IRAs
